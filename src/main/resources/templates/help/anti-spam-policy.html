Anti-Spam Policy

What is spam?

In the context of electronic messaging, spam refers to unsolicited, bulk or indiscriminate messages, typically sent for a commercial purpose.


Handemdowns has a zero-tolerance spam policy.


Automated spam filtering

Handemdowns messaging systems automatically scan all incoming email and other messages, and filter-out messages that appear to be spam.


Problems with spam filtering

No message filtering system is 100% accurate, and from time to time legitimate messages will be filtered-out by Handemdowns systems. If you believe this has happened to a message you have sent, please advise the message recipient by another means.

You can reduce the risk of a message being caught by the spam filters by sending the message in plain text (i.e. no HTML), removing any attachments, and ensuring that your messages are scanned for malware before dispatch.


User spam

Handemdowns provides a service that enables users to send email messages to others. Users must not use this service to send unsolicited, bulk or indiscriminate messages, whether or not for a commercial purposes. Full terms and conditions concerning the use of the facility are set out in Terms of Use.


Receipt of unwanted messages from Handemdowns

In the unlikely event that you receive any message from Handemdowns or sent using Handemdowns systems that may be considered to be spam, please contact Handemdowns using the details below and the matter will be investigated.


Changes to this anti-spam policy

Handemdowns may amend this anti-spam policy at any time by publishing a new version on this website.
